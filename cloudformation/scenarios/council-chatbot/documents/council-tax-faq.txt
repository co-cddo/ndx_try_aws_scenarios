SAMPLE COUNCIL - COUNCIL TAX FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: When is my council tax bill due?
A: Bills are issued in March for the financial year starting 1 April. If paying by direct debit over 10 months, the first payment is in April. You can also choose 12 monthly instalments.

Q: I've just moved in - what should I do?
A: Notify us within 21 days at www.samplecouncil.gov.uk/council-tax or call 01234-567-891. We will calculate your bill from the date you moved in.

Q: Can I pay my council tax weekly?
A: We offer monthly direct debit (10 or 12 instalments), but not weekly payments. You can make additional payments at any time online.

Q: My circumstances have changed - will this affect my bill?
A: Yes, changes such as someone moving in or out, a death, or starting or finishing full-time education can affect your bill. Contact us within 21 days.

Q: What happens if I don't pay?
A: We will send reminders. If you still don't pay, we apply to the Magistrates Court for a Liability Order (83 pounds court cost added). This allows us to deduct from wages or benefits, use enforcement agents, or in extreme cases apply for committal to prison. Please contact us early if you are struggling to pay.

Q: Can I get a reduction if I'm on a low income?
A: Yes, apply for Council Tax Support (up to 80 percent reduction for working age, up to 100 percent for pension age). Apply online at www.samplecouncil.gov.uk/council-tax-support.

Contact: counciltax@samplecouncil.gov.uk or 01234-567-891
