SAMPLE COUNCIL - PLANNING FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Do I need planning permission for a garden shed?
A: Usually no, if it meets Permitted Development rules: maximum 2.5m height, located behind the building line, and covering less than 50 percent of the garden. Different rules apply in conservation areas.

Q: How long does a planning application take?
A: Householder applications: 8 weeks. Minor applications: 8 weeks. Major applications: 13 weeks. These are statutory deadlines and most applications are decided within these timescales.

Q: Can I see planning applications for my area?
A: Yes, view the planning register at www.samplecouncil.gov.uk/planning-register. You can search by address, application number, or view weekly lists.

Q: My neighbour's extension blocks my light - can I object?
A: You can comment on any planning application during the 21-day consultation period. Loss of light is a material planning consideration. However, there is no legal right to light in planning law.

Q: Do I need permission to convert my garage?
A: If you are converting an integral garage to a habitable room, you usually need planning permission as it changes the front elevation. You will also need Building Regulations approval.
