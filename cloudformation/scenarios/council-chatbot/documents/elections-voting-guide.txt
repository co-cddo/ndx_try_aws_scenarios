SAMPLE COUNCIL - ELECTIONS AND VOTING GUIDE

Register to Vote
You must be registered to vote in elections. Register online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote. You need your National Insurance number.
Registration deadline: 12 working days before an election.
You can register if you are a British, Irish, or qualifying Commonwealth citizen aged 16 or over (you can vote when you turn 18).

Voting Methods
- In person: Vote at your polling station on election day (7am-10pm). Bring photo ID (since October 2023).
- Postal vote: Apply at www.gov.uk/apply-postal-vote. Applications must be received 11 working days before the election.
- Proxy vote: Appoint someone to vote on your behalf. Apply at www.gov.uk/apply-proxy-vote.

Accepted Photo ID for Voting
Passport, driving licence, blue badge, older person's bus pass, Voter Authority Certificate.
If you do not have accepted photo ID, apply for a free Voter Authority Certificate at www.gov.uk/apply-for-photo-id-voter-authority-certificate.

Upcoming Elections
Check www.samplecouncil.gov.uk/elections for details of upcoming elections in your area.
Council elections: Held in May. One third of councillors elected each year (no election in the fourth year).
Parish council elections: Held alongside council elections.

Becoming a Councillor
You can stand for election if you are 18 or over, a British/Irish/qualifying Commonwealth citizen, and live or work in the council area.

Contact: elections@samplecouncil.gov.uk or 01234-567-899
