SAMPLE COUNCIL - BUSINESS RATES GUIDE

What are Business Rates?
Business rates (National Non-Domestic Rates) are the way businesses and other non-domestic property occupiers contribute towards the cost of local services.

How Rates are Calculated
Your rates are based on the rateable value (RV) set by the Valuation Office Agency (VOA) multiplied by the rate multiplier set by government.
2024-25 standard multiplier: 51.2p. Small business multiplier: 49.9p.
Example: Property with RV of 20,000 pounds = 20,000 x 0.499 = 9,980 pounds per year.

Small Business Rate Relief
If your property has a rateable value below 15,000 pounds, you may qualify for Small Business Rate Relief (up to 100 percent for RV under 12,000 pounds, tapered to 0 percent at 15,000 pounds).
Apply: www.samplecouncil.gov.uk/business-rates-relief

Other Reliefs Available
Charitable relief: 80 percent mandatory plus 20 percent discretionary for registered charities.
Empty property relief: 3 months for commercial, 6 months for industrial.
Retail, hospitality, and leisure relief: 75 percent discount (current government scheme).
Hardship relief: Discretionary relief for businesses in financial difficulty.

Payment
Pay monthly by direct debit over 10 or 12 instalments. Online payment also available.
